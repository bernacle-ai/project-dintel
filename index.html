13 Know Before They Happen

Failure Modes & Counter-Strategies

Your team identified these. Pre-wire your response before each failure mode happens — not after.

❌ Failure Mode 1: The Price Race to the Bottom

You try to compete with Garuda Aerospace on per-acre flight costs. You lower your prices to win. You go bankrupt.

Counter-Strategy: Hold your premium pricing. You do not sell flights — you sell the API integration, the 24-hour SLA, and the compliance-ready report. If a client refuses: execute Plan C — offer to white-label your analytics engine to Garuda themselves. Turn a competitor into a revenue stream.

❌ Failure Mode 2: Platform Lock-In (Asteria SkyDeck)

You build your entire workflow inside Asteria's SkyDeck platform. They raise API prices or restrict access. Your margins vanish instantly.

Counter-Strategy: Keep your analytics stack hardware-agnostic and portable from Day 1. Use FlytBase (not SkyDeck) for fleet management. Use OpenDroneMap + QGIS (not Asteria's cloud) for processing. Buy their hardware if it's the best option. Never let any vendor own your intelligence layer.

❌ Failure Mode 3: Data Breach or NPNT Violation

A freelancer leaks client spatial data, or a rented drone enters a Red Zone without NPNT digital permission. DPDP Act fine up to ₹250 crore. DGCA suspension. Your company is finished.

Counter-Strategy: Enforce mandatory pre-flight Digital Sky checklist on every rental operator you use. Implement strict RBAC — freelancers get watermarked data environments, never raw client data. AWS S3 Object Lock for all logs. If breach occurs: activate your CERT-In incident response playbook (you must build this before you need it).

❌ Failure Mode 4: Cash Flow Collapse (Government Slow Payment)

You win a large government contract in Month 4. The work is done. Payment comes in Month 11. Your burn rate eats your capital. You cannot pay the freelancer or the rental.

Counter-Strategy: Balance portfolio: minimum 50% private enterprise clients (15–45 day payment) at all times. Use CGSS collateral-free working capital to bridge government payment gaps. Udyam EMD exemption prevents upfront capital drain on bid deposits. Never let government projects exceed 50% of active revenue pipeline in Year 1.

❌ Failure Mode 5: GIS Freelancer Becomes a Bottleneck

You get 5 projects simultaneously. Your one GIS engineer is overwhelmed. Delivery slips. Clients cancel. Reputation damaged before it's even built.

Counter-Strategy: Onboard and vet 5 freelance GIS engineers from Day 1 — not one. Build strict SOPs and standardized Turbo Report templates so any trained engineer can produce consistent output. Appoint a QA lead whose only job is verification. By Month 7–8, hire the best freelancer as your first full-time GIS analyst.

❌ Failure Mode 6: Scope Creep Destroys Your Margins

Client says "just one more analysis" after the project is delivered. You say yes to be nice. You do free work for 3 extra weeks. ₹75,000 project becomes a ₹15,000 effective rate.

Counter-Strategy: Fixed-scope contracts for every engagement. Every deliverable listed with a line item. Change requests = new purchase order, minimum ₹15,000. State this in your proposal template. Clients who respect your work will respect your scope. Those who don't were never your customers anyway.
